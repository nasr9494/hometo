Pyramids of Meroë

Located in the northeastern part of Sudan, the Pyramids of Meroë are an incredible UNESCO World Heritage site. These pyramids are part of the ancient Kingdom of Kush and are a stunning testament to Sudan's ancient civilization. The site features over 200 pyramids, many of which are smaller and steeper than those in Egypt.

Khartoum

Khartoum, the capital of Sudan, is where the Blue Nile and White Nile converge. It's a city rich in history and culture, with many museums, mosques, and bustling markets. Visitors can explore the National Museum of Sudan and the Al-Mogran Family Park, or take a boat ride along the Nile.

Jebel Barkal

Jebel Barkal is a mountain located near the ancient city of Napata, and it's another UNESCO World Heritage site. The mountain is home to the ruins of a temple dedicated to the god Amun, and it is believed to have been a major religious and political center in the Kingdom of Kush.

Sennar Dam

The Sennar Dam, located on the Blue Nile, is an important landmark for Sudan's water management system. It's not just a vital infrastructure project but also a scenic spot. Visitors can enjoy beautiful views of the surrounding landscapes and the vast expanse of the Nile River.

Red Sea Coast

The Red Sea coast of Sudan is an untouched paradise. With beautiful beaches, vibrant coral reefs, and a diverse marine life, it's an ideal destination for diving and snorkeling. The port city of Port Sudan is a great base for exploring the area.
